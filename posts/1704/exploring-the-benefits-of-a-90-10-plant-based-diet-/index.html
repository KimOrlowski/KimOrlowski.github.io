It serves as an effective way to adopt a more plant-based lifestyle without becoming fully vegan. The diet is based on research that plants are the optimal source of micronutrients and macronutrients for maintaining good health and nutrition. This diet encourages more nutrient-dense plant-based foods while maintaining a balance of macronutrients from animal-based sources. Additionally, this approach offers many health benefits, including improved heart health, easier digestion, and weight management. It also reduces the environmental burden of animal agriculture and contributes to sustainability. Q1: What is a 90/10 Plant-Based Diet?

A1: A 90/10 plant-based diet is a nutrient-dense, whole-foods diet with a focus on plant-based foods. It consists of 90% plant-based foods, such as fruits, vegetables, legumes, nuts, seeds, and whole grains, and 10% of animal-based proteins, such as fish, eggs, and dairy.

Q2: How is it beneficial to our health?

A2: A 90/10 plant-based diet has numerous benefits for our health. Studies have shown that it can reduce the risk of chronic diseases, such as type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and certain types of cancer. It can also help to reduce inflammation and improve digestion. Additionally, it is a sustainable way of eating, as it has a lower environmental impact than diets that are high in animal-based proteins.

Q3: What should I eat on a 90/10 Plant-Based Diet?

A3: On a 90/10 plant-based diet, you should focus on eating whole, unprocessed foods that are high in fiber and nutrient-dense. Your meals should consist of 90% plant-based foods, such as fruits, vegetables, beans, legumes, nuts, seeds, and whole grains, and 10% of animal-based proteins, such as eggs, fish, and dairy.

Q4: Are there any potential risks associated with this diet?

A4: Although a 90/10 plant-based diet offers numerous benefits for our health, there are some potential risks associated with following this diet. People who eat a completely plant-based diet can be at risk of developing a vitamin B12 deficiency. Additionally, people who eat a lot of processed plant-based foods may also be at risk of developing other nutrient deficiencies.

Q5: What tips do you have for transitioning to a 90/10 Plant-Based Diet?

A5: To make transitioning to a 90/10 plant-based diet easier, start by slowly incorporating more plant-based meals into your diet. Focus on eating whole, unprocessed foods, such as fruits, vegetables, nuts, seeds, legumes, and whole grains. Additionally, a good way to ensure you’re getting enough protein is to incorporate plant-based proteins into your diet, such as quinoa, amaranth, tofu, and tempeh. Finally, make sure to consult with your healthcare provider before making any significant changes to your diet.
