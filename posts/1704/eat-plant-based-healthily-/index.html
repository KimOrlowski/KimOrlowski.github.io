1. What is a 90/10 Plant-Based Diet?#

The 90/10 plant-based diet is a style of eating that focuses on a healthy balance between plant-based foods and animal-based foods. The diet requires that 90% any given meal be plant-based while the remaining 10% is up to the individual. The idea behind this diet is that having 90% of your meals plant-based can lead to improved overall health. Eating a variety of nutrient-rich plant-based foods can help the body in many ways, including providing essential vitamins, minerals, fiber and other important nutrients. Animal-based proteins are also beneficial, but this diet suggests limiting the animal-based foods to only 10% of your meals. This approach can help to reduce unhealthy saturated fats and cholesterol that are often found in animal-based foods. With an emphasis on plant-based foods, the 90/10 diet may help reduce the risk for certain chronic health conditions, such as heart disease and cancer.

Q3: What are some tips for transitioning to a 90/10 plant-based diet?

A3: Transitioning to a 90/10 plant-based diet can be an exciting and rewarding experience. Here are a few tips to help make the transition easier:

  1. Start by slowly adding more plant-based foods to your diet. Focus on whole grains, legumes, fruits, vegetables, and nuts to get the most nutrition.

  2. Aim to eat a variety of plant-based foods to ensure you get all the nutrients you need.

  3. Choose healthy fats like olive oil, flaxseed oil, and avocados and limit unhealthy fats like trans fats and saturated fats.

  4. Take your time. Don’t try to rush the transition. Give yourself time to adjust and find recipes that work for you.

Q4: What foods are off-limits on the 90/10 plan?

A4: While there are no foods that are completely off-limits on the 90/10 plant-based diet, it is important to limit consumption of animal-based foods. It is recommended to limit consumption of red meat, processed meats, dairy-products, and eggs to 10% or less of the diet. Additionally, it is important to limit consumption of processed foods, white refined flour, and sugar, as these are not part of a healthy diet.
