1. Examining the Impact of War on the Characters#

This article examines the impact of war on the characters of the classic novel “All Quiet on the Western Front.” The horrors of war take a clear toll on the characters of the novel, causing them to make difficult choices and to experience disparate psychological effects. Many of the characters are pulled into enlisting into the war effort, including the protagonist, Paul Baumer. He and his friends find it all but impossible to fight for a country that seems increasingly out of touch with reality and that is unable to recognize the consequences of their decisions. Despite a visceral desire to survive the war, the men come to understand the vulnerability of life and the futility of war. The loss of life and innocence are clearly displayed in the novel, leaving readers with a grim reminder of the cost of war. Paul eventually finds himself alone, the only one of his original group to survive the war, and his sense of loneliness and despair is palpable. “All Quiet on the Western Front” offers a powerful and truthful glimpse into the lives of the characters and the impact of war on their lives.
