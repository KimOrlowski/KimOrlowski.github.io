Do you suffer from digestive problems or discomfort? Are you considering trying the Low FODMAP diet but not sure where to start? Join us as we explore the common mistakes to avoid when following this diet, and the tips and tricks you need to know to achieve success. When it comes to starting a low FODMAP diet, it is easy to make mistakes that can set us back. It is important to remember that it’s okay to make mistakes, but being aware of common errors can help us avoid them and make our dietary journey smoother. Here are some common mistakes to avoid on a low FODMAP diet.

One common mistake to avoid is not consulting a dietician or doctor before starting a low FODMAP diet. This diet is not for everyone and requires specialised knowledge in order to create a balanced and healthy meal plan. Consulting a professional can help immensely when it comes to understanding which foods are in the low FODMAP category and how to ensure a nutritionally balanced diet that meets all of our needs.

Another mistake to avoid is eating too little food. A low FODMAP diet consists of foods that can be digested easier, however, it is still important to ensure we are taking in enough calories and nutrients to meet our daily requirements. If we are not, we may find ourselves feeling fatigued and unwell.

Finally, it is important to ensure we are only avoiding foods in the high FODMAP categories, not all FODMAPs. While some FODMAPs can irritate our intestines, others are beneficial, aiding in digestion and providing us with essential vitamins and minerals. If we limit our diet to only low FODMAP foods, we may leave ourselves short on important nutrients.

In conclusion, while starting a low FODMAP diet can be beneficial for some, it is important to know and understand the common mistakes to avoid in order to get the most out of our new dietary lifestyle. By consulting a professional, eating a balanced diet and only avoiding high FODMAP foods, we can ensure a positive and successful journey.

  1. Excessively Eliminating FODMAPs - According to a study published in the journal Nutrients, excessive restriction of FODMAPs can lead to imbalances in gut bacteria, cause nutritional deficiencies, and lead to adverse health effects. Registered dietitian, Erin Macdonald, recommends that people “seek out a dietitian with experience in FODMAPs to ensure they are still able to meet their micronutrient needs”.

  2. Following a Low-FODMAP Diet Indefinitely - FODMAPs are natural components of food and needed for proper digestion. According to a research review in the journal Nutrients, results from studies suggest that it is best to follow a low-FODMAP diet for no more than two to six months at a time, and then reintroduce higher-FODMAP foods as tolerated.

  3. Skipping the Introduction Phase - Registered dietitian Rachel Paul says “a low FODMAP diet is not a one-size fits all approach and there’s an introduction phase that’s necessary to determine which FODMAP’s are trigger foods for the individual.” She says it is important to work with a registered dietitian to make sure that the diet is individualized and the reintroduction phase is done safely.

By following a Low FODMAP Diet, you’ll be able to reduce the amount of gas and bloating in your body, as well as reduce the frequency of diarrhea in some cases. Secondly, remember to read food labels carefully. Many foods that are labeled Low FODMAP can include ingredients that are not allowed such as garlic and onion. Thirdly, be sure to keep a food diary.
