- Overview of Anti-Inflammatory Diet Plan#

An anti-inflammatory diet plan is becoming increasingly popular as a way to promote overall good health and reduce disease risk. Inflammation is a normal function of the body’s immune system, however chronic inflammation is linked to a number of serious diseases. By focusing on certain foods and avoiding others, it is possible to achieve long-term health benefits by following an anti-inflammatory diet plan.

The main focus of an anti-inflammatory diet plan is to limit or eliminate processed foods and focus on whole foods such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes, nuts, seeds and lean proteins. These whole foods are low in saturated fats and trans fats which are known to contribute to inflammation. Additionally, it is recommended to limit foods that are high in added sugar, such as sweets, cakes and sugary beverages.

In addition to consuming whole foods, supplementing with anti-inflammatory herbs and spices is recommended for further reducing inflammation. Finally, an anti-inflammatory diet plan calls for the inclusion of healthy fats, which are found in nuts, seeds, olive oil, and omega-3 fatty acids from fish such as salmon and sardines. Omega-3 fatty acids are known to reduce inflammation and offer a wide variety of health benefits. By following an anti-inflammatory diet plan, it is possible to achieve long-term health benefits.
